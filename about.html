About Eighred

Manifesto

Our guiding principles are set in stone.

Privacy

Personal privacy is a fundamental human right and states have an obligation to respect it. Mass surveillance and the collection of personal data threaten the foundations of a democratic society. That's why we are building an untraceable internet. We provide the highest level of protection for your payments, messaging and online activities. Unless you choose to tell them for some mysterious reason.

Strong Encryption and Communication Privacy

Internet users should protect communication privacy by using strong encryption technologies. Strong encryption secures individuals' freedom of expression and personal privacy.

Open and global internet

It is the most powerful tool for communication and collaboration we have ever seen. Some of our greatest hopes for human progress are taking shape online. It makes it easier for us to learn new things, reminds us that we share humanity, and helps solve many of the problems facing people around the world.

Over the past decade we have seen these good things happen in many ways. But we have also seen the power of the internet used to fuel discrimination, incite violence, spread hate speech and deliberately manipulate the truth. We have learned that we need to articulate more clearly what we yearn for about people's lives online, and that is what we are doing now.

Freedom

It is a principle that does not restrict the behavior of individuals unless it violates the rights of other people. This principle is one of the cornerstones of modern democracies. People can think, believe, express and act freely, but these freedoms must not jeopardize the rights or safety of others.

Freedom includes setting the boundaries of personal space and the right of individuals to control their own lives, thoughts and actions. However, these freedoms may be limited by the need to protect the rights of other members of society and ensure their safety. In other words, personal freedoms must be balanced with the general well-being and peace of society.

The exercise of freedoms must therefore be regulated in a fair and balanced manner to protect the rights and security of other members of society. This is a fundamental part of society's social contract and we aim to create an environment in which everyone enjoys justice, equality and respect for human rights.

Independent

We believe in staying true to these principles. That's why we are 100% supported by our users, not investors.
